How to read this

              
This view estimates annual OT loss exposure using low, likely and high event/loss assumptions. It is useful when risk stakeholders want a range-based discussion rather than a single ROI estimate.

              
The reduction is linked to the selected solution mix and discounted by control confidence. It is not a formal FAIR or actuarial model.

            

          

        


        

          

            

              
Advanced Input Guidance

              
What To Enter In The Risk Simulation

            

            Examples included
          


          

            

              
ROI calculator

              
Purpose: build a business case from agreed planning inputs.

              
Use when: finance can provide downtime cost, investment and a reasonable expected incident pattern.

              
Output: annual benefit, avoided downtime, payback and cumulative ROI.

            

            

              
Risk simulation

              
Purpose: explore uncertainty and loss exposure ranges.

              
Use when: risk owners want low, likely and high assumptions, or the business case needs P90/P95 exposure values.

              
Output: baseline loss, residual loss, probability of at least one event and mean annual risk reduction.

            

          


          

            

              
Threat Scenarios

              
Count the distinct OT cyber scenarios in scope, such as ransomware in production, infected engineering workstation, remote access compromise or segmentation failure.

              
Example: use 4 if the workshop covers ransomware, malware via USB, unauthorized remote access and supplier compromise.

            

            

              
Simulation Trials

              
This controls how many local browser samples are used. Higher values make the output smoother but are still only an estimate.

              
Example: 1,000 is fine for workshops. Use 5,000-10,000 if presenting a more stable P90/P95 view.

            

            

              
Events Per Year

              
Enter a minimum, likely and maximum annual frequency for OT-impacting cyber events. Keep near misses separate unless risk stakeholders explicitly want them included.

              
Example: 0.5 / 2 / 5 means one event every two years at minimum, two expected events, and five in a high-pressure year.

            

            

              
Loss Per Event

              
Enter the low, likely and high loss if one OT-impacting event occurs. Include downtime, recovery, external response, overtime, scrap and restart cost if those are not modelled elsewhere.

              
Example: 150,000 / 650,000 / 1,800,000 for a site where short outages are common but a major production stop is possible.

            

            

              
Control Confidence

              
This discounts the selected solution mix to reflect real-world deployment quality, coverage, monitoring maturity and operating discipline.

              
Example: use 50-60% for early deployment assumptions, 70-80% for validated coverage, and avoid 85%+ unless evidence is strong.

            

            

              
Shared Naming

              
Annual benefit belongs to the ROI calculator. Annual risk reduction belongs to the risk simulation.

              
Use the ROI calculator for investment return. Use risk simulation for exposure language such as baseline, residual, P90 and P95 loss.

Calculation Transparency

              
How The Numbers Are Calculated

            

            Model logic
          


          

            

              
Scenario Adjustment

              
The selected product risk reductions are combined first, then adjusted by scenario.

              
Conservative = combined reduction x 0.70

              
Expected = combined reduction x 1.00

              
Aggressive = combined reduction x 1.20

              
The final scenario reduction is capped at 75% to avoid unrealistic full-risk removal.

            

            

              
Product Combination

              
Product effects are not added directly. The model compounds remaining risk, so selecting several products does not create impossible reductions above 100%.

              
Combined reduction = 1 - ((1 - product A) x (1 - product B) x ...)

              
Example: 20% and 30% do not become 50%. They become 44%, because the second control reduces the remaining risk.

            

            

              
Legacy Multiplier

              
Legacy OT systems increase downtime and recovery exposure because they are harder to patch, rebuild and operate safely during incidents.

              
Legacy multiplier = 1 + (legacy percentage x 0.35)

              
At 40% legacy, the multiplier is 1.14. This is intentionally moderate and should be validated with OT and risk stakeholders.

            

            

              
Avoided Downtime

              
The downtime benefit estimates how much expected production loss is avoided by reducing OT cyber incident risk.

              
Annual downtime exposure = incidents/year x downtime hours/incident x downtime cost/hour x legacy multiplier

              
Avoided downtime = annual downtime exposure x scenario reduction

            

            

              
Reduced Incident Loss

              
This covers direct incident recovery cost, not downtime. Downtime and recovery should stay separate to avoid double counting.

              
Annual recovery exposure = incidents/year x recovery cost/incident x legacy multiplier

              
Reduced incident loss = annual recovery exposure x scenario reduction

            

            

              
Operational Efficiency

              
This estimates time saved for OT/security teams through fewer manual checks, fewer exceptions, faster response and better control visibility.

              
Annual FTE cost = FTE count x hourly FTE cost x 2,080

              
Efficiency rate = (scenario reduction x 0.10) + environment scale

              
The efficiency rate is capped at 22%.

            

            

              
Annual Benefit

              
The main benefit is the sum of avoided downtime, reduced recovery loss and operational efficiency saving.

              
Annual benefit = avoided downtime + reduced incident loss + operational efficiency saving

              
This should be treated as an estimate for business case discussion, not a guarantee.

            

            

              
Payback

              
Payback shows how long it takes for the estimated benefit to equal the investment.

              
Payback months = (investment amount / annual benefit) x 12

              
If annual benefit is zero, the model shows no payback.

            

            

              
Net Value And ROI

              
Net value and ROI use the selected calculation period. The default is 3 years.

              
Period net value = (annual benefit x calculation period) - investment amount

              
Period cumulative ROI = period net value / investment amount x 100

              
This is cumulative ROI over the full period, not annual ROI.

            

            

              
Risk Simulation

              
The risk simulation tab uses low, likely and high ranges for annual event frequency and loss per event. It samples values inside those ranges to estimate a distribution.

              
Baseline loss = sampled annual events x sampled loss per event x scenario complexity

              
Residual loss = baseline loss x (1 - confidence-adjusted reduction)

            

            

              
Control Confidence

              
Advanced risk reduction is discounted by confidence to reflect deployment quality, scope and operational maturity.

              
Confidence-adjusted reduction = combined product reduction x control confidence

              
The risk simulation caps this at 70%.

            

            

              
Important Boundary

              
This is not an actuarial, FAIR-certified or insurance-grade risk model. It is a transparent workshop model for early business case estimation.

              
All inputs, assumptions and outputs should be validated with finance, OT, security and risk teams before external use.

            

          

        

      


      

        

          

            

              
Workshop Method

              
Which Numbers To Use

            

            Finance validated
          


          

            

              
Environment Size

              
Sites should be physical plants, factories, labs, warehouses or production locations included in scope.

              
Production lines should count lines where downtime has measurable production, safety or logistics impact.

              
OT endpoints should include HMIs, engineering workstations, OT servers, fixed-function Windows systems and other protected industrial endpoints.

            

            

              
Legacy Exposure

              
Use the percentage of OT endpoints or systems that are difficult to patch, unsupported, running legacy OS versions or operationally constrained.

              
Use a conservative estimate if the inventory is incomplete. Do not include normal IT endpoints unless they are part of the OT scope.

            

            

              
Downtime Cost

              
Use finance-approved cost per hour. Include lost production margin, scrap, restart labor, missed SLA penalties and emergency logistics where applicable.

              
If the customer does not know the number, ask finance or plant operations for a low, expected and high estimate before using the output externally.

            

            

              
Incident Frequency

              
Incidents per year should be based on the last 24-36 months of OT-impacting malware, ransomware, unauthorized access, infected media, segmentation failures or recovery events.

              
For a first workshop, separate confirmed OT incidents from near misses and use the lower confirmed number in the conservative case.

            

            

              
Recovery Cost

              
Use the average direct recovery cost per incident: internal labor, external response, rebuilds, forensic support, expedited spares, validation and overtime.

              
Do not double count downtime cost here. Keep downtime in the downtime fields and recovery cost in this field.

            

            

              
Operational Efficiency

              
FTE count should include people who spend time on OT security operations, patch exceptions, malware handling, incident response, asset checks or compensating controls.

              
Hourly FTE cost should be fully loaded cost if finance can provide it. If not, use salary plus benefits and overhead divided by annual working hours.

            

            

              
Investment

              
Include software, hardware, services, deployment, support and internal project effort if the customer wants a full business case.

              
Use the same currency across all inputs. Select the display currency in the calculator before copying the executive summary.

            

            

              
Risk Reduction Assumptions

              
Product percentages are editable assumptions, not guarantees. Use lower values until the customer validates coverage, deployment scope and operating model.

              
A good starting point is to keep expected combined reduction below 30-40% unless there is strong evidence from incidents, controls mapping or validated risk analysis.

            

            

              
Risk Simulation

              
Use the risk simulation tab when the customer wants range-based risk discussion. Minimum, likely and maximum values should come from incident history, plant recovery exercises, insurance analysis or risk workshops.

              
Control confidence should reflect deployment coverage, asset visibility, policy maturity, monitoring quality and operational adoption.

            

            

              
Scenario Discipline

              
Conservative should be defensible to finance. Expected should represent the agreed planning case. Aggressive should be used only for upside discussion.

              
Before sharing externally, validate assumptions with finance, OT operations and risk owners.
