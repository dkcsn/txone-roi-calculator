How to read this

              
This view estimates annual OT loss exposure using low, likely and high event/loss assumptions. It is useful when risk stakeholders want a range-based discussion rather than a single ROI estimate.

              
The reduction is linked to the selected TXOne solution mix and discounted by control confidence. It is not a formal FAIR or actuarial model.

Workshop Method

              
Which Numbers To Use

            

            Finance validated
          


          

            

              
Environment Size

              
Sites should be physical plants, factories, labs, warehouses or production locations included in scope.

              
Production lines should count lines where downtime has measurable production, safety or logistics impact.

              
OT endpoints should include HMIs, engineering workstations, OT servers, fixed-function Windows systems and other protected industrial endpoints.

            

            

              
Legacy Exposure

              
Use the percentage of OT endpoints or systems that are difficult to patch, unsupported, running legacy OS versions or operationally constrained.

              
Use a conservative estimate if the inventory is incomplete. Do not include normal IT endpoints unless they are part of the OT scope.

            

            

              
Downtime Cost

              
Use finance-approved cost per hour. Include lost production margin, scrap, restart labor, missed SLA penalties and emergency logistics where applicable.

              
If the customer does not know the number, ask finance or plant operations for a low, expected and high estimate before using the output externally.

            

            

              
Incident Frequency

              
Incidents per year should be based on the last 24-36 months of OT-impacting malware, ransomware, unauthorized access, infected media, segmentation failures or recovery events.

              
For a first workshop, separate confirmed OT incidents from near misses and use the lower confirmed number in the conservative case.

            

            

              
Recovery Cost

              
Use the average direct recovery cost per incident: internal labor, external response, rebuilds, forensic support, expedited spares, validation and overtime.

              
Do not double count downtime cost here. Keep downtime in the downtime fields and recovery cost in this field.

            

            

              
Operational Efficiency

              
FTE count should include people who spend time on OT security operations, patch exceptions, malware handling, incident response, asset checks or compensating controls.

              
Hourly FTE cost should be fully loaded cost if finance can provide it. If not, use salary plus benefits and overhead divided by annual working hours.

            

            

              
Investment

              
Include software, hardware, services, deployment, support and internal project effort if the customer wants a full business case.

              
Use the same currency across all inputs. Select the display currency in the calculator before copying the executive summary.

            

            

              
Risk Reduction Assumptions

              
Product percentages are editable assumptions, not guarantees. Use lower values until the customer validates coverage, deployment scope and operating model.

              
A good starting point is to keep expected combined reduction below 30-40% unless there is strong evidence from incidents, controls mapping or validated risk analysis.

            

            

              
Advanced Risk Model

              
Use the advanced tab when the customer wants range-based risk discussion. Minimum, likely and maximum values should come from incident history, plant recovery exercises, insurance analysis or risk workshops.

              
Control confidence should reflect deployment coverage, asset visibility, policy maturity, monitoring quality and operational adoption.

            

            

              
Scenario Discipline

              
Conservative should be defensible to finance. Expected should represent the agreed planning case. Aggressive should be used only for upside discussion.

              
Before sharing externally, validate assumptions with finance, OT operations and risk owners.
